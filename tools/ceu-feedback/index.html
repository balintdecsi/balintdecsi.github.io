Compliance & Privacy

Built to meet CEU and EU standards

FeedForward is designed from the ground up to comply with GDPR, CEU's Data Protection Policy, and Austrian education law — making university approval straightforward.

Regulatory framework

Compliance at every layer

🇪🇺

GDPR — Art. 6(1)(e)

Processing is based on a public-interest task — improving the quality of higher education. Data is minimised, and students are informed of their rights at registration.

🔐

Data Minimisation

Only the email address is collected to verify eligibility. After verification, it is hashed and not stored alongside any feedback record. Feedback contains no PII.

👤

Full Anonymisation

Submitted feedback is irreversibly decoupled from any student identity before persistence. Reports expose only aggregated statistics — never individual responses.

🏛️

Institutional Oversight

The platform is operated under the authority of the CEU Academic Council. Dashboard access is restricted to authorised academic staff per department.

🗂️

Data Retention Limits

Raw feedback data is retained for no longer than 5 years from collection, after which it is permanently deleted. Students may request early deletion at any time.

🛡️

Access Controls

Role-based access control ensures that professors cannot access their own individual feedback records, only aggregated reports prepared by the Quality Assurance Office.

✍️

Student Rights

Students retain the right to access, rectify, and erase their data at any time. A dedicated data-subject request portal is accessible from every page.

🔑

@student.ceu.edu Only

Registration is restricted to the official CEU student email domain. This prevents external actors from submitting feedback and ensures all participants are current students.

📋

Audit Trail

All administrative actions (access grants, report exports, deletion requests) are logged in an immutable audit trail available to the Data Protection Officer.

Data Protection Notice

⚠️ This is a MVP demo for university review purposes. The notice below reflects the intended production policy.

Data Controller: Central European University, Quellenstraße 51, 1100 Vienna, Austria.

Purpose: To collect anonymous structured feedback from enrolled students to support teaching quality assurance and academic programme improvement.

Legal basis: GDPR Article 6(1)(e) — processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest (higher education quality assurance).

Data collected: Student email address (for eligibility verification only; not retained with feedback), ratings (1–5 per dimension), and optional free-text comments. No name, student ID, or demographic information is collected.

Anonymisation: Email addresses are used only to verify eligibility and prevent duplicate submissions. After submission, no linkage is maintained between feedback and the submitting student.

Recipients: Aggregated, anonymised reports are accessible to the Academic Council and, where relevant, department heads. Individual responses are never shared.

Retention: Feedback data is retained for five (5) academic years, after which it is permanently deleted from all systems.

Your rights: You have the right to access, rectify, erase, or restrict the processing of your data, and to lodge a complaint with the Austrian Data Protection Authority (dsb.gv.at). To exercise these rights, contact dpo@ceu.edu.

About the project

Why FeedForward?

A proposal to replace ad-hoc, paper-based evaluation forms with a digital-first, privacy-respecting platform that actually gets used.

The problem

Current CEU end-of-term evaluation forms suffer from low completion rates, generic questions, and no mechanism for students to track whether their feedback was acted upon. Anonymity guarantees are unclear, reducing trust and the quality of input.

The solution

FeedForward provides a dedicated, mobile-friendly web platform accessible with existing CEU student credentials. It separates professor and course feedback, uses structured rating dimensions that map to CEU quality criteria, and provides real-time anonymity guarantees backed by technical controls.

University approval pathway

📝

Phase 1 — Pilot

A controlled pilot with two departments and volunteer professors. Feedback collected under a full DPIA. Results shared with Academic Council.

🔍

Phase 2 — Audit

Independent security and privacy audit by CEU's Data Protection Officer. Integration with CEU SSO for seamless authentication.

🚀

Phase 3 — Rollout

University-wide deployment, replacing existing paper forms. Ongoing monitoring and a Student Advisory Board to guide future improvements.
